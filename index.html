Background & Motivation

About This Research

What Are Hybrid Android Apps?

Android hybrid apps combine native Android components with embedded web technologies, typically via WebView-based APIs. This design choice enables cross-platform, rapid development and is commonly used in domains such as healthcare and banking apps, where mobile access to information and services is critical.

The Research Gap

Prior research has explored the prevalence, characteristics, and security concerns of Android hybrid applications. However, current empirical knowledge remains fragmented. Most large-scale studies focus on observable behaviors or architectural properties, while comparatively little attention has been paid to how hybrid app technologies affect the software development and maintenance process itself.

In particular, there is limited understanding of the types of software issues that developers encounter due to hybrid-specific mechanisms such as:

Sensitive Domains Focus

Safety- and privacy-critical domains such as healthcare and public services remain underexplored. These domains are subject to stricter correctness, reliability, and privacy requirements, making hybrid-app-specific issues potentially more impactful.

Positioning

Related Work

This project builds on existing empirical and security research on Android hybrid applications, including large-scale studies of Android‚Äìweb hybridization and analyses of specific hybrid-related risks such as fingerprinting, tracking, and privacy leakage.

üí°

Complementary Perspective

While prior work has provided important insights into the prevalence, architecture, and runtime behavior of hybrid apps, this project adopts a complementary perspective by focusing on developer-facing artifacts and maintenance processes.

Key Contribution

The contribution of this project is therefore not the discovery of hybrid app risks in general, but the construction of a curated dataset and the systematic characterization of hybrid-app-specific development issues in sensitive domains, complementing existing behavioral and architectural studies.

Research Questions

Focus: Quantifying the prevalence of hybrid-related issues
RQ2 Categories

What categories of hybrid-specific issues are most commonly reported?

  • WebView configuration
  • Java‚ÄìJavaScript interfaces
  • Lifecycle mismatches
RQ3 Manifestation
